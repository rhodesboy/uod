1. Choose your Course

We have over 200 full-time courses for Undergraduate Study. Choose a course using our Course Search then apply through UCAS. Still can't decide? Join our Visit/Open Day.

2. Register with UCAS

Register with the UCAS Apply online tracking application. The Introduction to Apply video guide shows you how to fill in your application.

Additional information about applying to specific courses, Part-time, Distance Learning and Study Abroad courses can be found on individual course webpages.

Key Dates

Apply a year before you wish to start university:

Applications begin 1 Sept
Applications closing date 15 Jan
Medicine or Dentistry closing date 15 Oct

Late applications received at UCAS between 16 January and 30 June will be considered at the discretion of the university.

If you plan to take a year out, apply two years in advance of starting university.

UCAS Key Dates for Undergraduates

3. Your application

UCAS will send your online application to our Admissions office. A minimum of two staff members go through the information you have provided to get an understanding of your motivation for choosing your course at the University of Dundee.

4. Interviews, Portfolios and Selection Tests

Read our individual course webpages to find out if your chosen course requires an interview, portfolio or selection test.

We will provide guidance of what is expected of you once we have made our initial assessment after the UCAS deadline.

There are no set dates or times for interviews or tests, but generally they take place between January and April. If there are dates when you are unavailable for interview (e.g. due to examinations) you can mark these in the appropriate space on your UCAS application.

5. Our Offer

UCAS allows our admissions tutors to make three types of offers of admission:

Unconditional Offer
If you have already obtained the necessary entry qualifications.
Conditional Offer
if you still need to obtain the passes/grades specified in the offer, in examinations still to be taken, to secure entry. If you obtain these passes, your offer will change to become an unconditional offer, meaning you definitely have a place. To secure a place, all conditions of an offer must be met by 31 August. This date refers also to applicants who have deferred their entry.
Rejection
if you are not suitably qualified.

UCAS have a useful video on how to Make sense of your offers

We will make our decision after the 1 February. This is to ensure we consider all applications equally that apply by the UCAS deadline.

If you apply for Level 2 or Level 3 entry to a course but you do not meet the conditions of the offer, you will not be automatically considered for entry to a lower Level of entry as there may no longer be vacancies.

6. Received an Offer?

Congratulations! Successful applicants (those receiving an offer of admission) have the opportunity to join a Post-Application Visit before replying to their offer.

 

No offers? Missed the deadline?

UCAS Extra

You can choose up to 5 courses on your UCAS Application.

If you have used up all of your original 5 choices, but are holding no offers, UCAS Extra will give you the opportunity to make further applications and receive an offer before the exam results are published and Clearing starts. UCAS will write to you if you are eligible for Extra.

Clearing

Our clearing service operates from 1 August until the end of September. You can use Clearing if:

  • you're not accepted on a course
  • you decline all offers
  • or you applied after 30 June

If you still want to go to University the same year, add a clearing choice on your UCAS application. The Clearing (how to) guide explains what you need to do.

Transferring from another University

Applicants wishing to transfer from another University must apply through UCAS.
